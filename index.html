Mauryan Art and Administration

  1. Ashoka set up many stupas, viharas, rock and pillar edicts and around his empire.
  2. The tall and highly polished monolithic pillan of Ashoka are very important. The most famous Ashokan pillar is at Sarnath.

Lion Capital

  • This is the lion capital which depicts four lions seated back to back and facing all four directions.
  • The abacus is wonderfully carved and various animals such as elephant, bull, lion and horse are depicted on it. .

Ashoka Chakra

  1. The Ashoka Chakra is found on the Indian flag and the Sarnath lion symbol is the national emblem of India.
  2. Below the lion capital it is depicted the Ashoka Chakra or the Wheel of Dharma .

Ashokan Pillar

  • The Ashokan pillars can be divided into two parts-the shaft or the lower part and the capital or the top part of the pillar.
  • The capital can further be divided into three parts-the animal sculpture on top, the abacus and an inverted lotus below it.

Sanchi Stupa

  1. The Great Stupa at Sanchi is one of the oldest stone structures in India, and an important monument of Indian Architecture.
  2. > It was originally commissioned by the Mauryan emperor Ashoka the Great in the 3rd century BCE.
    3. Its nucleus was a simple hemispherical brick structure built over the relics of the Buddha. .

Decline of Mauryan Empire

  • Ashoka died in 232 BCE. After his death, the empire began to disintegrate. He was succeeded by weak and inefficient rulers who were unable to control the vast empire.
  • In 185 BCE, the last ruler of the Mauryas. Brihadratha, was killed by Pushyamitra Sunga who founded the Sunga dynasty.
